FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Recipes before you download.

Is Recipes free to download?
Yes, Recipes is completely free to download on the App Store for iPhone and iPad. Optional in-app purchases are available at $0.99/month or $9.99/year for anyone who wants to support ongoing development and unlock additional features.
Can I import recipes from any website?
Yes. The built-in recipe importer works with thousands of popular cooking websites including AllRecipes, Food Network, NYT Cooking, Serious Eats, Bon Appétit, and many more. Just paste the URL into the app and it automatically extracts the title, ingredients, and steps — no manual entry required.
Does the app sync across my iPhone, iPad, and Mac?
Yes. Recipes uses iCloud to keep your recipe collection perfectly in sync across all your Apple devices. Any recipe you save on your iPhone appears instantly on your iPad and Mac, and vice versa — with no setup required beyond signing into iCloud.
Do I need to create an account?
No account is required. Recipes uses your existing iCloud account for sync and storage, which means there's nothing to sign up for and no password to remember. Just download and start cooking.
Does Recipes collect my personal data?
No. The developer collects absolutely zero data from users. Your recipes are stored privately in your own iCloud account and are never shared with third parties, advertisers, or anyone else. Recipes is one of the most privacy-friendly cooking apps on the App Store.
Can I use Recipes to build a grocery list?
Yes — and it's one of the best features. From any saved recipe, you can add all ingredients directly to your grocery list with a single tap. The app consolidates ingredients from multiple recipes and organizes them by category, so shopping is faster and you never forget anything.
What iPhone and iPad models are supported?
Recipes requires iOS 16 or later on iPhone and iPadOS 16 or later on iPad. It also runs on Mac via Apple Silicon (M1 chip or later). It is optimized for both iPhone and iPad with a multi-column layout for larger screens.

About

The Best Way to Manage Recipes on iPhone

What Is Recipes?

Recipes is a free recipe organizer and digital cookbook designed specifically for iPhone and iPad. It lets you save recipes from any website on the internet, organize them into custom folders, and cook from a clean, distraction-free interface — all synced automatically across your Apple devices via iCloud. Whether you're looking for the best recipe keeper app for iPhone, a simple way to organize your family's favorite dishes, or a smarter approach to grocery shopping, Recipes was built for exactly that purpose.

Why Home Cooks Prefer a Dedicated Recipe App Over Bookmarks or Notes

Most people start organizing recipes with whatever's handy — a folder of browser bookmarks, screenshots saved to their camera roll, or a running list in the Notes app. These approaches work at first, but they break down quickly. Websites go offline. Screenshots pile up with no way to search them. Your Notes app becomes an unsearchable wall of text. A dedicated iPhone recipe app solves all of these problems at once by giving every recipe a permanent, structured, searchable home on your device.

  • Saved permanently: Even if the original website disappears, your imported recipe stays safe in the app.
  • Works offline: Access any saved recipe without an internet connection — perfect for cooking in the kitchen.
  • Fully searchable: Find any recipe by title, ingredient, or cuisine in seconds.
  • Ad-free: No pop-ups, no auto-playing videos, no distractions — just the recipe.

How to Organize Your Recipe Collection on iPhone

The most effective recipe organization system is one that mirrors how you actually think about food. Recipes lets you create unlimited custom folders, so you can structure your cookbook exactly the way you want. Common approaches include organizing by meal type (Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner, Snacks), by occasion (Weeknight Dinners, Meal Prep, Holiday Baking), by cuisine (Italian, Mexican, Asian), or by cooking method (Instant Pot, Air Fryer, Grilling). You can also maintain a "Want to Try" folder for recipes you've saved but haven't cooked yet, and a "House Favorites" folder for the dishes your household requests most often.

Importing Recipes from the Web to Your iPhone

One of Recipes's most powerful features is its recipe importer, which works with thousands of popular food and cooking websites. Instead of bookmarking a page and hoping it's still there when you need it, the importer extracts the recipe content and saves it directly to your collection in a clean, standardized format. This means no more ads, no more scrolling past lengthy blog introductions, and no more lost recipes when websites go dark. The importer works with sites like AllRecipes, Food Network, Serious Eats, NYT Cooking, Bon Appétit, The Kitchn, and thousands more.

Building Smarter Grocery Lists from Your Recipes

Recipes bridges the gap between meal planning and grocery shopping in a way that standalone shopping list apps can't. Because your recipes and your grocery list live in the same app, you can add all the ingredients from a recipe to your shopping list with a single tap. Plan your meals for the week, add ingredients from each dish, and the app consolidates everything into a single categorized list — grouping produce, proteins, dairy, pantry staples, and other departments together so you move through the store efficiently without retracing your steps.

Privacy and Security: Your Recipes Are Yours

Unlike many recipe apps that require creating an account and storing your data on third-party servers, Recipes uses iCloud — Apple's secure cloud platform — for all storage and sync. This means your recipes are stored in your own iCloud account, governed by Apple's world-class privacy policies. The developer collects zero data from users, which means no analytics on what recipes you save, no behavioral tracking, and no data sold to advertisers. For privacy-conscious iPhone users, Recipes is the clear choice among recipe organizers on the App Store.
